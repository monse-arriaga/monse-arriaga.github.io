¡Hola!

I'm Monse Arriaga — I study how intelligent systems learn, reason, and see the world.

I'm a Computer Science student at the National Autonomous University of Mexico (UNAM), fascinated by how machines learn to perceive and make sense of the world. My work moves between academia and industry — research collaborations on one side, hands-on technology projects on the other — and I'm happiest working somewhere in that overlap.

I'm especially drawn to computer vision, multimodal AI, world models, and representation learning, along with the trickier question of whether we can really trust and interpret what these systems do. I also study English Literature, which keeps me thinking about language and meaning from a completely different direction.

Outside of research I'm usually deep in a long playthrough of Baldur's Gate.
